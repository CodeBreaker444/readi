## What's New

### PIC-Technician — A New Promotion for Pilots

Not every pilot carries out physical maintenance work, and not every pilot who does needs the same level of access. To bridge this gap, we've introduced a **Technician promotion** that can be added on top of any standard PIC account.

When a pilot is granted the Technician promotion:

- They can see their assigned maintenance tickets in the Maintenance section.
- They can log when they **start** and **end** their work on a ticket (intervention tracking).
- They can **upload supporting documents** (photos, checklists, inspection forms) directly to the ticket.
- They can **submit an intervention report** summarising what was done.

What they cannot do:

- Assign tickets to themselves or others.
- Close a ticket — only a Maintenance Manager, Admin, or authorised manager can do that.
- Download the ticket's file archive (this remains a manager-level action).

The promotion can be granted by any RM, Admin, or Super Admin from the **Edit User** panel. It also appears during user creation — if you're adding a new PIC and you already know they'll be handling maintenance, you can tick the box and they'll be set up with the promotion from day one.

---

### Intervention Tracking — Start and End Work on a Ticket

Once a pilot has the Technician promotion and is assigned to a ticket, they'll see two new action buttons on that ticket:

- **Start** — marks the moment the technician begins work. The ticket moves to *In Progress* automatically.
- **End** — marks when the work is complete and the technician steps back.

Both timestamps are logged against the ticket and visible in the event history. This gives managers a clear, timestamped record of how long work actually took, and confirms when hands-on intervention happened.

---

### Maintenance Tickets Now Visible to Assigned PIC Users

Previously, the Maintenance section was not accessible to PIC users at all. Now, when a PIC has the Technician promotion (or when a ticket is assigned to them), they can navigate to the Maintenance section and see only the tickets assigned specifically to them — nothing else is visible.

The interface adapts to their role:

- The **Assign** button is hidden — they cannot reassign tickets.
- The **Download Files** button is hidden — document download is for managers.
- The **Event History** is fully visible so they can see the full context of a ticket.
- The **Close Ticket** button is not shown — only managers can close.

---

### Revoking the Technician Promotion is Protected

If a manager tries to remove the Technician promotion from a pilot who still has an open or in-progress ticket assigned to them, the system will **block the revoke** and show a clear message explaining which ticket needs to be closed first.

This prevents a situation where a pilot's access is removed mid-job, leaving a ticket without an active technician. Once the relevant ticket is closed by a manager, the promotion can be revoked without issue.

The blocking message includes the specific ticket number so managers know exactly what to act on.

---

### Close Ticket Now Works on In-Progress Tickets

Managers can now close a ticket even when it is in the *In Progress* state — for example, if a technician forgot to tap End, or if the ticket needs to be wrapped up urgently. A clear warning is shown inside the close dialog when the ticket is still in progress, so the manager is always aware before confirming.

---

## Improvements

- The **New Ticket** button in Maintenance is now hidden for PIC users — ticket creation remains a manager-level action.
- The sub-role section in the Edit User panel only appears when managing a PIC account, keeping the interface clean for other roles.
- When creating a PIC user, the sub-role section appears automatically as soon as the PIC role is selected, so managers don't miss the option.
- Battery logbook expanded view now shows a sequential cycle number per mission and displays the cycle ratio value clearly as `1.00 cycle ratio` for easier reading.

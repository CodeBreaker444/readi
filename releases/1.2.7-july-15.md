## What's New

### More Drone Details Come Across From D-Flight

When you import a drone from D-Flight, or link an existing component to one, Readi now also pulls in its UAS Serial Number, GCS Serial Number, and License Plate — alongside the D-Flight ID and insurance details it already brought over.

### Find and Link a Drone by Its License Plate

You no longer need to go through the D-Flight Fleet page to line up a drone with Readi. From the Add or Edit Component screen, just type in the drone's license plate and Readi will look it up in D-Flight and fill in the matching details for you.

### Automatic Brand & Model Matching on Import

When importing a drone from D-Flight, Readi now looks up its brand and model and matches it to your existing registry automatically — or gives you a ready-made starting point to create a new one if nothing matches yet.

### Enter ENAC Authorizations and STS Declarations by Hand

Until D-Flight makes this information available automatically, you can now record a drone's ENAC Authorizations and STS Declarations yourself when adding or editing a drone component.

### Insurance Reminders You Can Configure

Each component's insurance record can now have its own list of people to notify and its own reminder window. As a policy's expiry date gets close, the right people get an in-app notification, and anyone who logs in while it's still unread sees a pop-up reminder.
